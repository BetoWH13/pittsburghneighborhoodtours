Pittsburgh is a city steeped in history, and what better way to experience that history than by staying in one of its iconic historic hotels? From grand Beaux-Arts landmarks to charming Victorian bed and breakfasts, these hotels offer a unique window into the city's past while providing all the comforts of the present.

## 1. Omni William Penn Hotel

**Location:** 530 William Penn Pl, Pittsburgh, PA 15219

### Overview
Opened in 1916, the Omni William Penn Hotel is a grand icon of Pittsburgh's history. Designed by the renowned architects Benno Janssen and Franklin Abbott, this hotel has hosted countless celebrities, politicians, and dignitaries over the past century.

### Historical Significance
- **Architectural Marvel:** The hotel showcases Beaux-Arts architecture with ornate details, grand chandeliers, and opulent ballrooms.
- **Speakeasy History:** During Prohibition, a secret speakeasy operated in the hotel's basement. Today, you can visit the Speakeasy lounge, which pays homage to that era.
- **Famous Guests:** Notable figures like John F. Kennedy, Lawrence Welk, and Elvis Presley have stayed here.

### Modern Amenities
- Luxurious guest rooms with modern furnishings.
- Multiple dining options, including the Terrace Room, offering fine dining in an elegant setting.
- Fitness center and complimentary Wi-Fi.

## 2. The Renaissance Pittsburgh Hotel

**Location:** 107 6th St, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

### Overview
Set in the historic Fulton Building constructed in 1906, the Renaissance Pittsburgh Hotel combines old-world charm with contemporary luxury. The building's distinctive copper dome and neoclassical architecture make it a city landmark.

### Historical Significance
- **Architectural Beauty:** The hotel retains many original features, including marble staircases, vaulted ceilings, and intricate mosaics.
- **Cultural Proximity:** Located in the Cultural District, the hotel is surrounded by theaters and galleries that have been part of Pittsburgh's artistic heritage.

### Modern Amenities
- Stylish rooms with river or city views.
- On-site restaurants serving local cuisine.
- Fitness facilities and event spaces.

## 3. The Priory Hotel

**Location:** 614 Pressley St, Pittsburgh, PA 15212

### Overview
Originally built in 1888 as a Benedictine monastery, The Priory Hotel is a boutique establishment that offers a unique and intimate experience. It's a family-owned hotel that exudes historical charm.

### Historical Significance
- **Monastic Origins:** The building served as a monastery for German monks and retains its Gothic architectural elements.
- **Historic Renovation:** Restored in the 1980s, the hotel preserves its historical integrity while providing modern comforts.

### Modern Amenities

## Tips for Experiencing Pittsburgh's History

- **Explore Beyond the Hotels:** Visit nearby historical sites like the Fort Pitt Block House, the oldest architectural landmark in Pittsburgh, and the Cathedral of Learning at the University of Pittsburgh.
- **Guided Tours:** Many hotels offer or can arrange for historical tours of the city.
- **Cultural District:** Stay in or visit the Cultural District to enjoy theaters, galleries, and performances that celebrate Pittsburgh's artistic heritage.
- **Local Cuisine:** Dine at restaurants that have been part of the city's history, like Primanti Bros., famous for its sandwiches since 1933.
- **Transportation:** Utilize the city's historic inclines, the Duquesne and Monongahela Inclines, for panoramic views and a touch of history.
