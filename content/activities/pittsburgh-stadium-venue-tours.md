## PNC Park Tours

### Why PNC Park Tours Are Worth It

PNC Park opened in 2001 and immediately set the standard for what a modern baseball stadium could be. The right field upper deck view — Downtown Pittsburgh skyline visible over the outfield wall, the Roberto Clemente Bridge in the foreground, the Allegheny River behind it — is routinely cited as the single best view in American professional sports.

A behind-the-scenes tour takes you to parts of the park unavailable on game days:

**What PNC Park tours typically include:**
- **Press box and broadcast booths** — the view from which games are called
- **Dugout and on-field access** — standing in the Pirates dugout, walking on the warning track
- **Clubhouse** — the Pirates locker room (when the team is on the road)
- **Premium club areas** — the Clemente Museum-level club and field-level suites
- **The Roberto Clemente Bridge approach** — the pedestrian bridge becomes a natural part of the tour story

**Booking:** Tours are offered on non-game days and select game days. Check the Pittsburgh Pirates official website for the current tour schedule and booking. Tours fill up; book at least a week ahead for summer dates.

**Duration:** Approximately 75–90 minutes

**Cost:** Modest fee; children under a certain age may be free — check current pricing

### Roberto Clemente Museum

Technically separate from PNC Park but spiritually connected — the Roberto Clemente Museum in Lawrenceville is the definitive tribute to the Pirates' Hall of Fame right fielder, who died in a plane crash on New Year's Eve 1972 while delivering humanitarian aid to Nicaragua. The museum is housed in a converted firehouse and requires advance reservation. For anyone interested in Pittsburgh baseball history, this is the more moving experience.

## Acrisure Stadium Tours (Formerly Heinz Field)

### Behind the Scenes at Steelers Country

Acrisure Stadium opened in 2001 alongside PNC Park as part of Pittsburgh's North Shore sports complex development. The stadium holds 68,400 people and has hosted two Super Bowls. The Steelers' six Super Bowl championships — more than any other franchise — give the building a weight of history that a tour makes tangible.

**What Acrisure Stadium tours typically include:**
- **Field level access** — standing on the same turf where the Steelers play
- **Steelers locker room** — when the team is out of season or on the road
- **Press level and broadcast areas** — the view from which 70,000 Steelers fans appear as a single yellow mass
- **Club level suites** — premium areas with full city views
- **The Hall of Honor** — the Steelers' historical display of six Lombardi Trophies and franchise milestones

**Booking:** Tours run year-round on non-game days. Check the Steelers website or Acrisure Stadium's official tour page for current scheduling. The offseason (February–July) typically offers more availability.

**Duration:** Approximately 75 minutes

**A note on the name:** The stadium was known as Heinz Field from 2001–2021 before the naming rights changed. Locals still use both names interchangeably.

## Pittsburgh University Campus Tours

Pittsburgh's universities are architecturally extraordinary and worth visiting as destinations in their own right.

### University of Pittsburgh

Pitt's Cathedral of Learning is one of the most remarkable university buildings in the world — a 42-story Gothic skyscraper (the tallest educational building in the Western Hemisphere) containing Nationality Rooms: 30 classrooms each designed in the architectural style of a different country, donated by Pittsburgh's immigrant communities. The Commons Room at the base of the tower is open to the public. Free guided tours of the Nationality Rooms are available.

**Location:** Fifth Avenue and Bigelow Boulevard, Oakland
**Tours:** Public tours run most days; check Pitt's visitor center for current schedule
**Cost:** Free for self-guided; nominal fee for guided Nationality Rooms tour

### Carnegie Mellon University

Carnegie Mellon's campus sits adjacent to Pitt in Oakland, designed around a central mall by Henry Hornbostel. The College of Fine Arts building (1912) is architecturally significant; the university's robotics and computer science facilities are open to prospective students and occasionally to the public through specific programming.

**Location:** Forbes and Fifth Avenues, Oakland — adjacent to the Carnegie Museums

## Historic Performance Venue Tours

### Heinz Hall

The Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra's home on Penn Avenue in the Cultural District is a 1927 movie palace converted to concert use in 1971. The interior — Baroque Revival plasterwork, the chandelier, the ceiling — is worth experiencing even without a concert. Pre-concert tours are occasionally offered; the venue hosts open houses and educational programming periodically.

### Benedum Center for the Performing Arts

The Benedum (formerly the Stanley Theatre, 1928) is Pittsburgh's premier performing arts venue and one of the great surviving movie palaces in the country. The Pittsburgh Ballet Theatre, Pittsburgh Opera, and Broadway touring productions perform here. Architecture tours are occasionally offered — check Pittsburgh Cultural Trust programming.

## Frequently Asked Questions

#### Do I need to book in advance?
Most outdoor activities and self-guided options require no advance booking. For popular restaurants, museum tickets on busy weekends, or stadium games, booking ahead is strongly recommended.

#### Is Pittsburgh easy to navigate as a first-time visitor?
Yes, with some planning. Downtown and the North Shore are very walkable. The East End neighborhoods are best reached by bus or car. Pittsburgh's geography — hills, bridges, rivers — is part of the experience, not an obstacle.

#### What is the best time of year to visit Pittsburgh?
Late spring (May–June) and fall (September–October) offer the best weather and the most outdoor events. Summer brings festivals and baseball. Winter is cold but the holiday lights along the river are genuinely beautiful.
